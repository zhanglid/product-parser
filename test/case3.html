Sneaky PotatoTop Contributor: Fountain Pens
5.0 out of 5 starsI Didn't Know Good Combs Were A Thing
August 26, 2017
Size: 9T|Verified Purchase
**8 Month Update**

Unsurprisingly, this comb has held up amazingly well over the past eight months. The gold lettering on the comb itself has faded and worn away, but not a single tooth has broken. I haven't been exactly gentle with it, either, since I take it just about everywhere I go and travel often. I definitely recommend it, and I've even broken down and purchased one of their expensive beard brushes as well because of the quality of the comb.

-------------------------------------------------------------

I've been using the same kind of comb for years, and I really didn't think that I needed anything better. My biggest gripe was the fact that the teeth tended to break often, so I decided to try something new. I try to get high-quality versions of things in my life that I use daily, and I feel like this was definitely a good buy.

First off, this comb is probably the right size for you, unless you're used to really, really big combs. It's kind of confusing when trying to compare the sizes. This isn't a pocket comb, but very much like the standard combs I'm used to using. It's quite a bit thicker and heavier, but the teeth spacing is about identical. I can use the fine side on my beard, and the regular side on my head hair.

I'll be honest, I was a bit underwhelmed when I first opened the packaging. It's encased in a kind of cheap-feeling plastic sleeve, and the lettering on the comb looks pretty generic and blah. I know that it seems crazy to even point that stuff out, but this is more expensive than the combs we are all probably used to using, so I feel like some better packaging would be nice. The comb itself has a nice smooth feeling and weight in the hand. It's very pleasant to hold. Surprisingly, it feels quite nice to comb your hair with this. I mean, it's still just a comb at the end of the day, but it definitely feels better than the cheap combs I was previously using. No, it didn't cure world hunger or make my hair look any different, but it's nice to know that I'm not shredding apart my hair with cheaper plastic every day.

I would definitely keep buying these combs, especially since they feel like they're built to last. If you're going to comb your hair every day, you might as well get a good one.
